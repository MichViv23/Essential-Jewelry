Some of the materials we use

•Diamonds

Diamonds are one of the hardest natural substances found on earth. All of our natural diamonds are within the GHI color range and SI-I 1-2 clarity. In addition, our trusted partners only use Kimberley Process-compliant natural diamonds. The Kimberley Process (KP) is a multilateral trade regime established in 2003 with the goal of preventing the flow of conflict diamonds. The core of this regime is the Kimberley Process Certification Scheme (KPCS) under which States implement safeguards on shipments of rough diamonds and certify them as “conflict free". Today, participants actively prevent 99.8% of worldwide trade.

•Gold

Solid gold is a precious metal that will not oxidize or discolor. 14k gold has a higher percentage of alloyed metal making it more durable and resistant to everyday wear than 18k gold. Our 14k white gold pieces are finished in rhodium plating, which gives them extra shine and durability. To help reduce our impact on the environment while still supporting local communities that rely on mining, all our pieces are made from 95% recycled gold and 5% newly mined gold (including 14k gold and 18k gold vermeil). There is no difference in quality between recycled and newly-mined gold.

•Silver

We use 925 sterling silver, the industry standard in the United States and most world markets. Made of 92.5% pure silver, it's a highly durable material ideal for daily, long-term wear. In 2022, we worked with our suppliers to produce 92% of our silver jewelry from recycled sources. This recycled percentage highlights our continued efforts to reduce our impact on the environment and increase traceability of precious materials across our supply chain.
